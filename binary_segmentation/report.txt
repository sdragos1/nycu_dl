# Technical Summary

## Implementation Highlights
This project was implemented in PyTorch with TensorBoard for experiment.
The pipeline includes model definition, training/validation loops, and inference with CSV export.
- Model: UNet & ResNet34
- Loss: BCEWithLogitsLoss + Dice loss
- Optimizer/Scheduler: Adam with OneCycleLR
- Checkpointing: best model selected by validation Dice score

## Data Preprocessing Strategy
Data preprocessing was implemented with torchvision.transforms.v2.
- Trimap masks were converted to binary masks by mapping foreground pixels to 1 and all others to 0.
- Images were normalized using ImageNet statistics.
- Training augmentation included RandomResizedCrop, RandomHorizontalFlip, RandomRotation, and ColorJitter.
- Validation/test preprocessing used deterministic transforms only resize and normalization without augmentation.

## Training Strategy
Training used configurable split files for train/validation and mini-batch optimization.
- 50 training epochs
- Mixed monitoring through validation loss and Dice score
- TensorBoard logging for training loss, validation loss, and learning rate
- Training performed on an RTX 4070 (8GB VRAM)

## Key Observations
- BCE + Dice produced better quality than BCE or CE alone.
- Selecting checkpoints by validation Dice produced models with better inference than selecting by validation loss only.
- OneCycleLR improved convergence speed and training stability compared to plateau scheduling.
